  • Aug 11, 2025

    Week33 Monday

    1 ubiquitous

    [ ju:ˈbɪkwɪtəs ]
    (v.) - present everywhere
    Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society.

    2 exacerbate

    [ ɪgˈzæsəbeɪt ]
    (v.) - to make a problem worse
    Poor urban planing exacerbates traffic congestion.

    3 ephemeral

    [ ɪˈfemərəl ]
    (adj.) - lasting for a very short time
    Social media trends are often ephemeral.

    4 pragmatic

    [ prægˈmætɪk ]
    (v.) - dealing with things sensibly and realistically
    A pragmatic approach to education focus on practical skills.

    5 resilient

    [ rɪˈzɪliənt ]
    (adj.) - able to recover from difficulties
    Children are often more resilient than adults think.

    6 detrimental

    [ ˌdetrɪˈmentl ]
    (adj.) - harmful or damaging
    Lack of sleep can be detrimental to health.

    7 inevitable

    [ ɪnˈevɪtəbl ]
    (adj.) - certain to happen, unavoidable
    Technological advancement is inevitable in the 21st centuary.

    8 conducive

    [ kənˈdju:sɪv ]
    (adj.) - making a certain situation likely or possible.
    A quiet environment is conducive to effective studying.

    9 paradigm

    [ ˈpærədaɪm ]
    (n.) - typical example or pattern
    The internet caused a paradigm shift in communication.

    10 precursor

    [ pri:ˈkɜ:sə(r) ]
    (n.) - something that comes before
    The telegraph was a precursor to modern communications.

    Week32 Friday

    1 mitigate

    [ ˈmɪtɪgeɪt ]
    (v.) - make less severe
    Planting trees helps mitigate the effects of climate change.

    2 proliferation

    [ prəˌlɪfəˈreɪʃn ]
    (n.) - rapid increase
    The proliferation of fast food has contributed to health problems.

    3 precarious

    [ prɪˈkeəriəs ]
    (adj.) - dangerously unstable
    Many workers face precarious employment conditions.

    4 corroborate

    [ kəˈrɒbəreɪt ]
    (v.) - confirm with evidence
    The witness testimony corroborated the defendant's story.

    5 discretionary

    [ dɪˈskreʃənəri ]
    (adj.) - available for optional use
    The company offers discretionary bonuses for exceptional work.

    6 exemplary

    [ ɪgˈzempləri ]
    (adj.) - serving as a desirable model
    The student showed exemplary behavior throughout the year.

    7 facilitate

    [ fəˈsɪlɪteɪt ]
    (v.) - make easier
    Technology facilitates global communication.

    8 inconsequential

    [ ɪnˌkɒnsɪˈkwenʃl ]
    (adj.) - unimportant
    The spelling mistake was inconsequential to the overall meaning.

    9 novel

    [ ˈnɒvl ]
    (adj.) - new and original
    The scientist proposed a novel approach to the problem.

    10 stringent

    [ ˈstrɪndʒənt ]
    (adj.) - strict and precise
    The university has stringent admission requirements.
